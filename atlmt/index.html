ATL Money Transfer “Website Rethinking”

ATL Money Transfer's is visited by more than 12000+ users per month, serving across 15+ countries. ATL platform enables migrants, expatriates and global citizens to access multi-currency, Send, Receive, Payment instantly from your phone.
In compliance with my NDA, I have omitted confidential information. The designs are a reinterpretation of the original.

The challenge

Educate without being annoying

I was tasked with improving the What’s New experience in the M365 Admin Dashboard. What’s New is a link in the toolbar that directs users to an article outlining new features and updates, and we wanted to educate users in a better way.

Competitive Analysis

ATL Money Transfer as a international Remittance service has many popular competitors that it has to face. for this reason, to begin my research, I looked at a few competitors to see where ATL stands relative to them. I analyzed their UI, UX, Information architecture, User Flow and overall features.

User's Needs

I identified different scenarios users find themselves in when ever they want to make use of any remittance service, so as be able to understand their perspectives and desired outcome.

  • My brother just asked me for some money so he can be able to clear his dues in school, i need to send him money for that.
  • I work in a different country and need to send money to my old parents back home.
  • Needs to know the charges just by landing into the home page.

Project Outcome

We are currently in the process of analysing data and gathering additional feedback from ATL Money Transfer to get a full picture of how the new integration is performing.

What did I learn?

  • I merely wanted to try out my hands on a better solution that can Provide digital access to core remittance service. but at the course of my research, I discovered more reasons why users deserve to have amazing experiences even while transacting. Also, I have learned more about the general digital remittance industry.
  • Secondly, it is difficult to change how people use the app, and rather than introduce an entire solution to a problem, sometimes it’s best to enhance the existing structure and utilize UX patterns that users are familiar with.
